Being a vegetarian or vegan helps the environment by using less resources. For example, the production of meat use more resources in terms of cattle feed, energy, and human labor. Harvesting  vegetables will reduce waste. The proper crops will also feed more people. However, being vegetarian requires more vigilance in terms of sufficient vitamins, which meat eaters need not concern. Overall, being vegetarian is easier on the environment because production of fruit, vegetables and grain relieve strain on resources and is a healthier life style.
